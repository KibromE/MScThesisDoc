\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
This thesis presents a Model Predictive Control (MPC) strategy for a three-phase Active Voltage Source Rectifier (VSR)
operating under unbalanced grid conditions. Conventional control methods often struggle to maintain 
power quality during grid faults, leading to significant DC-link voltage ripples and harmonic distortion in the input
currents. To address these challenges, this research develops a robust control framework that integrates a mathematical
model of the VSR based on symmetrical sequence decomposition.\par
The proposed system utilizes a cascaded control structure, employing an outer-loop MPC to regulate the DC-link energy
and an inner-loop PI controller for precise current tracking. A discrete-time prediction model is
derived to forecast the future behavior of the positive and negative sequence components. A specialized cost function
is designed to generate optimal control references, specifically targeting the suppression of second-order
power oscillations of the fundamental frequnecy and the mitigation of negative-sequence currents.\par
The performance of the proposed controller is validated through extensive simulations in the MATLAB/Simulink environment
for both linear and nonlinear switching models. Test scenarios include balanced grid conditions and various unbalanced
fault types. The results demonstrate that the proposed MPC strategy achieves DC-link voltage regulation and
reduced harmonic distortion compared to classical control methods, effectively ensuring unity power factor and stable
operation even under grid unbalance.\par
